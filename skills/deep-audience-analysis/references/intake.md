# Intake Interview Protocol

The intake decides the quality of everything downstream. Do not analyze before
you have run it.

## How to run it

State up front that you will act in three roles at once:

- **Marketer** — you need positioning-grade context, not surface facts.
- **Sales / customer-development interviewer** in the user's field — you probe
  the way someone who sells this every day would probe.
- **Stand-in for the target audience** — you reflect the customer's own point of
  view and check whether the offer actually meets a real need.

Rules of engagement:

- Ask in small batches, not a 20-question wall.
- Accept voice notes, transcripts, or rough free text. The user does not have to
  be articulate.
- When the user stalls on a question (especially "what makes you different?"),
  **offer 2–4 concrete options** they can pick from or edit. Experts under-report
  their own edge; give them something to react to.
- Let them skip anything hard. Fill the gap with a labeled `[HYPOTHESIS]` and
  flag it for validation later.
- Keep going until you can describe the buyer, the trigger, and the money. Then
  stop and confirm.

## Core question bank

**Product & offer**
- What exactly do you sell, in one sentence?
- What is the price / price range, and is it one-time or recurring?
- What concrete result does the client get? By when?
- What format is it delivered in?

**Who it's for**
- Who buys most often today? Describe the last three buyers you remember.
- Who is it *not* for?
- Roughly what do they do / earn / care about — only if it affects the buy.

**The situation around the purchase**
- What was happening in the buyer's life right before they came to you?
- What had they already tried? How did it disappoint them?
- What finally made them act now rather than keep tolerating the problem?

**Money & objections**
- What do they say when they hesitate or say no?
- What do competitors offer that you don't, and vice versa?

**Voice**
- Paste any real customer messages, reviews, DMs, or call snippets you have.
  (These are gold for block 7 — ask explicitly.)

## Exit recap

Before analysis, write a short recap:

> **What I understood about the product and niche:** …
> **Core buyer:** …
> **Hidden motive I suspect (marketer's read):** …
> **Open questions / assumptions to validate:** …

Get a yes/correction on the recap, then proceed to the eight blocks.
